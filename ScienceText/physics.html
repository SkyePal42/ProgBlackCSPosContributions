Physics - Enabling Experiments

As mentioned on the first page with references to the LHC and Curiosity rover, computers are often required to facililitate Physics experiments. With developments towards ever-smaller phenomena, computational detectors are needed to understand the minute differences which are the objects of study. However, this can also manifest on the macro scale, such as using computers to make tiny adjustments to the orientation of telescopes.

Furthermore, the acceptance of quantum mechanical theories introduce massive complexity and massive uncertainty, due to the nature of the theory being inherently probabilistic. Computer modeling and approximations allow us to gather solutions to otherwise intractable problems.
